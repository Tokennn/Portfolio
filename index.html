Hangman Project

One player chooses a word or phrase and draws a line of dashes to represent each letter of the word or phrase. Spaces between words can be indicated with longer dashes or blank spaces. The other player tries to guess the word or phrase by calling out letters. If the letter appears in the word or phrase, the first player writes it in the correct position(s) above the corresponding dash(es). If the letter does not appear in the word or phrase, the first player draws one part of a hangman diagram (see below). The second player continues to guess letters until they either correctly guess the word or phrase, or the hangman diagram is complete. If the second player guesses the word or phrase correctly, they win the game. If the hangman diagram is completed before the word or phrase is guessed, the first player wins. To create the hangman diagram, the first player draws a gallows with a hanging rope. Each time the second player guesses a letter that is not in the word or phrase, the first player adds a body part to the hangman. The order in which the body parts are typically drawn is: head, body, left arm, right arm, left leg, right leg.

Boxxle

The player must move all of the crates to designated spots on the grid, which are usually marked with some kind of indicator (such as a colored square). The player can only push one crate at a time, and cannot pull crates or move them sideways. The player must navigate around obstacles such as walls and other crates to reach the designated spots. The game is typically divided into levels, with each level presenting a new challenge and layout of crates, walls, and other obstacles. Some versions of the game may include additional elements, such as time limits, enemies to avoid, or power-ups that give the player special abilities.
